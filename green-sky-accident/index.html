Green Sky Accident

Green Sky Accident's Fever Dreams is easily my favorite album of 2015. It has to be the best unheard album of the year. Green Sky Accident lives in Bergen and doesn't get out of Norway much (at least to play music), so it's not surprising that this album hasn't topped any charts. But it's what I've listened to most this year and I'm not wrong in loving it.

After ten months of trying to distill what about this particular rock album puts it ahead of all rock albums I've got nothing. It's unabashedly guitar-driven. There's more than one decent singer. It's not afraid of sounding epic. It's not afraid of sounding like the kind of thing that would play during a montage on a network television drama (do these things still exist).

So I give up, and I give up in the only way I know how, which is with a hyperlink and an underhanded thank you to Eliot Van Buskirk for validating a love that has no justification. Here's what we hear when we listen to Green Sky Accident.
