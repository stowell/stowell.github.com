RIP Surfin' Obama, sometime 2013-early 2016

The end came before the end, watching the afternoon sun thicken, listening to Pudge relive every sensation of 1975. Who does that. You weren't even there. You're a bobble head.

The merch fades first, not long after the victory, certainly not beyond the first responsible decision taken, the first true believer disappointed. Surfin' Obama himself always had a tenuous relationship with Real Obama. Who could know for sure that citizenship doubts didn't drive sales? For his part, he steered clear of anyone who said POTUS without giggling like a fourteen year old boy. Most all of DC, really.

The worst thing about the end was how long it was. The glory days he could count on a balled-up fist. Measuring forty ounces of Bengali Tiger through a funnel. Hocking refrigerators, drums, IPAs, even a book shelf on Twitter. And what then? His head didn't really move all that much. No speaking tour beckoned. No leis. And what could he talk about for an hour fifteen, other than it's best when you don't let an actual bobble head run the country.

So you could say that whoever cleaved him in three did him a solid. Saved him from an uncertain irrelevance. More dust. Another talkative Hall of Famer. That's part of the truth. And yet, in any thick tragedy, they kill the clowns first. Hard to believe you've got the full weight of history behind you when there's a bouncing grin and a ludicrously large surfboard lurking behind the teleprompter.

ds, june tenth, two thousand sixteen
